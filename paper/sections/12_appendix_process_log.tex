The timeline contains one decisive contingency that is methodologically
worth flagging: the 2026-04-07 provenance audit, which discovered that
about half the original scrapers had been silently substituting
LLM-generated content for live source data, was the single most
expensive course-correction in the project (more than four days of
parallelised rebuild). It is the reason every fact-collection step in
the final pipeline (Section~\ref{sec:dataset}) emits a per-source URL
trace check, and it is the reason we recommend in
Section~\ref{sec:future} that any retargeting of the pipeline to
another expert domain run a comparable scraper-side provenance audit
before publication.
