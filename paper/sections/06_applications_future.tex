\section{Applications and Future Work}
\label{sec:future}

OenoBench is a benchmark first, but the construction pipeline behind
it is intended to be reusable. We outline three threads that build on
the released artifacts and methodology, in approximate order of
immediate feasibility.

\subsection{Closing the wine-knowledge gap in low-cost models}
The cost-vs-accuracy frontier (Section~\ref{sec:eval-cost}) shows that
small open-weight models trail proprietary frontier models by 20--30~pp
on this benchmark, even though the gap shrinks to 10--15~pp on broad
benchmarks like MMLU. The 38{,}104-fact corpus is a natural
\emph{supervised fine-tuning} target for a wine-specialist small model,
via a two-stage recipe: (i) instruction-style fine-tuning on a
held-out quartile of released question/source-fact pairs, teaching
the model to reason from a source fact; and (ii) LoRA adaptation on
the full 38{,}104-fact corpus, which the release schema exposes as
ready-made fact-question-source triples. Both stages run on consumer
GPUs without proprietary weights or pre-training compute. The
contextual slice (Section~\ref{sec:eval-cb}) is the harder-to-game
evaluation surface, so measured gain is a faithful proxy for genuine
wine reasoning. We expect a Llama-3.3-70B-class adaptation to recover
much of the GPT-5-mini gap at $\approx$1\% of proprietary inference
cost, opening cost-sensitive deployment paths for sommelier-tooling
and wine-education applications.

\subsection{Case-based evaluation with industry partners}
Multiple-choice tests have ceilings: a perfect OenoBench score does
not establish that a model can act usefully in real workflows. We
are scoping an \textbf{applied case-based dataset} with industry
partners along three personas:
\begin{itemize}\itemsep -1pt
  \item \emph{Sommelier $\rightarrow$ customer service:} consumer
    pairing prompts, rated by certified sommeliers against
    recommendation, justification, and substitutions.
  \item \emph{Winemaker $\rightarrow$ blending decisions:} vintage
    write-ups, lab analyses, and regulatory constraints fed in as
    context; outputs rated by working winemakers on technical
    correctness, regulatory compliance, and stylistic intent.
  \item \emph{Viticulturist $\rightarrow$ vineyard decisions:}
    multi-year weather, soil/disease pressure, canopy/yield records;
    canopy-management, irrigation, harvest-timing, and pest-control
    recommendations rated against decisions actually taken in the same
    vintage.
\end{itemize}
Such a dataset cannot be built from scraped facts alone --- it
requires expert annotation of free-form outputs --- but the OenoBench
construction pipeline (multi-strategy generation, multi-agent audit,
gold-sheet calibration) and the human-review app
(Appendix~\ref{app:screenshots}, already IRR-aware) carry over. We
treat OenoBench as the \emph{factual-recall floor} of an eventually
two-tier evaluation: factual knowledge must be near-saturated before
applied reasoning can be validly measured.

\subsection{Generalising the LLM-driven scrape-to-audit pipeline}
The pipeline elements --- (i) a tier-of-authority source taxonomy,
(ii) atomic-fact extraction with paraphrase enforcement,
(iii) multi-strategy multi-model generation with per-model quotas,
(iv) automated multi-agent audit calibrated against humans by
$\kappa$, and (v) a closed-book pre-screen that isolates parametric
leakage --- are domain-agnostic. They are implemented as separable
modules; swapping the source-tier configuration and the domain
taxonomy is sufficient to retarget. Three near-term targets share
wine's structural features: \emph{horticulture and agronomy}
(regulated appellations, peer-reviewed journals, certification
ladders); \emph{regulated medicine} (clinical guidelines, drug
formularies --- the strongest tier-of-authority structure of any
field, and where current benchmarks rely on board-exam questions
that are not aligned with practice); and \emph{financial regulation
and accounting} (FASB, IFRS, jurisdictional filings; CFA/CPA
ladders). In each case the released code reduces a months-long
expert-authoring effort to a weeks-long curation effort, provided
the tier-of-authority configuration is done carefully. Sections
\ref{sec:dataset} and \ref{sec:audit} are intended as a recipe for
that retargeting.
