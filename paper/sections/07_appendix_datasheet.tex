\label{app:datasheet}

We follow the datasheet template of \citet{TODOgebrudatasheets}.

\subsection{Motivation}
\paragraph{For what purpose was the dataset created?}
To evaluate factual knowledge of large language models on a multi-disciplinary, externally-validated domain (wine), and to provide a benchmark whose construction includes explicit bias controls (multi-model generation, multi-agent audit, self-preference scoring, expert-authored control set).

\subsection{Composition}
\paragraph{What do the instances represent?}
Each instance is a benchmark question with: question text, multiple-choice options (where applicable) with marked-correct answer, supporting fact(s) with source URL and tier, generator model identifier, generation strategy, audit-agent verdicts, and (for the gold-sheet subset) human reviewer ratings.

\paragraph{How many instances are there in total?}
\PLACEHOLDER{Final question count, target 5{,}000. Pilot at submission time: 146 questions in \texttt{audit\_pilot\_v13} (112 active, 34 \texttt{cb\_reserve}).}

\paragraph{What data does each instance consist of?}
Structured records over Postgres with columns described in Section~\ref{sec:dataset}.

\paragraph{Are there recommended data splits?}
Yes, four (Section~\ref{sec:eval}): full corpus, per-generator held-out, closed-book reserve, expert control.

\paragraph{Errors, noise, redundancies?}
Documented in Section~\ref{sec:audit}. Per-agent fail rates, $\kappa$ vs.\ gold sheet, and known agent calibration issues are released alongside the corpus.

\paragraph{Self-contained, or relies on external resources?}
Self-contained: question text, supporting facts, and source URLs are all included. Source URLs may rot over time; we mitigate by archiving Tier-1 source pages and recording the access timestamp.

\subsection{Collection process}
\paragraph{How was the data acquired?}
35 provenance-verified web scrapers (released as \texttt{src/scrapers/}). Sources span government registries, inter-governmental bodies (OIV), university research groups, Wikipedia/Wikidata, peer-reviewed journals, and curated open datasets. Scraping was rate-limited and used a research-disclosed user agent.

\paragraph{Time frame.}
Fact extraction: 2026-03 through 2026-04. Question generation: 2026-04 through 2026-05.

\subsection{Preprocessing / cleaning / labelling}
Atomic-fact extraction with entity tagging; per-source paraphrase to avoid verbatim copy; near-duplicate suppression; nine-agent automated audit; human gold-sheet rating on a 130-question subset.

\subsection{Uses}
\paragraph{Recommended uses.} Evaluation of LLM factual knowledge in the wine domain; ablation of bias-mitigation techniques in benchmark construction; study of self-preference effects in LLM-as-judge pipelines.
\paragraph{Discouraged uses.} Direct deployment as a wine-recommendation or purchasing system without separate alignment and age-gating; fine-tuning on the benchmark itself for ranking purposes (would invalidate the benchmark for the trained model).
